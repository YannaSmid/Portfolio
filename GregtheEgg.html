Greg the Egg

A gregarious artificial creature.

By Reyhan Danisan, Nikki Rademaker, and Yanna Smid

Project Overview

Meet Greg the Egg! A fluffy, pink creature with a playful personality and a need for constant interaction. Greg's personal playground is where he thrives, enjoying swings, merry-go-rounds, and seesaws. Greg’s charm lies in his ability to bring joy to anyone who plays with him, and in his sadness when left alone.

For the course Artificial Creatures, the challenge was to design and build a creature with a distinct personality using Arduino and minimal additional features. Our team aimed to create a lovable character that would connect emotionally with the audience.

Features

  • Interactive Behavior: Greg detects movement via an accelerometer, responding emotionally to interaction or lack thereof.
  • Physical Design: Soft pink yarn over a delicate eggshell, making Greg both cute and fragile.
  • Playground: A handmade swing, seesaw, and merry-go-round built to perfectly fit Greg.
  • Emotion Simulation: Greg “cries” when left alone for too long, emphasizing his need for companionship.

Technical Details

Greg the Egg's movements are detected through an accelerometer, that sends the data to the Arduino. The following materials were needed:

  • Hardware: Arduino Nano, accelerometer, and wiring.
  • Playground: Paint, paint stirring sticks, basket, hamster wheel, small easels, and green paper for the playground.

Challenges

  • Letting Arduino process and produce sounds is a more difficult task than anticipated. Arduino can only produce beeping sounds, with a low volume.
  • In the end, we managed to come up with two distinct beeping rhythms that can be recognized as happy and sad.

Process

  • Ideation: The first two weeks we brainstormed about ways to create a creature that emotionally connects with users.
  • Prototyping: After coming up with the idea of making a gregarious creature, we started sketching the design of the creature.
  • Development: When fully agreeing on the design, we integrated Arduino Nano and sensors to bring Greg to life. Moreover, we started crafting the playground.
  • Testing: When the first developments were done, we started testing the functions of Greg. We observed audience interaction to refine Greg’s responses.
  • Finetuning: After testing, observed mistakes or malfunctions were corrected to prepare Greg for the final showdown.
  • Exhibition: Finally, it was time to let Greg shine and we showcased him during an exhibition where audience was able to play with him and enjoy his creatureness.

Reflection

Greg the Egg highlights how creativity and technology can come together to evoke empathy and joy. The project reinforced the importance of designing with a user-centered and emotional perspective. Audience enjoyed playing with Greg, and were personally affected when leaving him behind.
