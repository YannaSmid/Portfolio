Four Seasons: Frostfire Mansion

Melt your way to the end of the mansion, to get to the next level.

By Yanna Smid

Project Overview

The Four Seasons Video Game is created for the course Introduction to Video Game Making. My Level, Frostfire Quest, is my contribution to a collaborative FPS microgame inspired by the theme of the four seasons. While others explored spring or summer, I dove deep into the quiet intensity of winter. I wanted to design a level that didn’t just look cold, but felt immersive and engaging to figure out, like a puzzle game.

Frostfire Quest is a first-person puzzle shooter inspired by the classic Zelda-style dungeon design. Built within Unity’s FPS Microgame framework, the level immerses the player in a snowy, abandoned winter house where the key to progress lies in solving environmental challenges, defeating enemies, and finding the legendary fire blaster, to melt the ice cubes that block your way to the next level.

Features

  • Seasonal Worldbuilding: The Video Game transitions from late autumn to a full-on winter theme, creating a coherent and immersive environment.
  • Puzzle-based Progression: Players must find and use the fire blaster to melt ice obstacles and unlock new paths.
  • Zelda-Inspired Mini Boss Battle: Classic structure with a challenging enemy encounter to earn the key item.
  • Environmental Mechanics: There are slippery ice floors, freezing cubes, and moving platforms. This makes the game more challenging.
  • Layered Level Design: There is a gradual introduction of mechanics and increasing difficulty, to keep the player engaged and learning.

Technical Details

  • Programming: Unity, C#
  • Leap Motion SDK: Used for capturing and translating hand gestures
  • Assets: Custom textures and winter-themed props, with Zelda-inspired sound effects for nostalgic flair
  • The FPS Microgame framework was used as a template
  • Play the prototype here: FourSeasons

Reflection

This project was an opportunity to mix mechanical design, level pacing, and thematic storytelling. This was the first Video Game I ever created, and I had found inspiration from my favorite Zelda games. I aimed to bring that structure into a modern FPS format. I learned how to design rewarding challenges, introduce mechanics through gameplay, and build levels that guide players intuitively. It also taught me how to balance difficulty—especially when combining puzzles, combat, and platforming. The result is a nostalgic, snowy puzzle adventure that encourages exploration, discovery, and a little bit of firepower. For future work I would like to adjust the difficulty of the game, as it can be frustrating to play due to the difficulty.
